https://www.gsa.gov/technology/government-it-initiatives/cybersecurity/cybersecurity-programs-and-policy General Services Administration Two proposed cybersecurity regulations under the Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) were recently published in the Federal Register. The first, FAR case 2021-017, attempts to improve the government's sharing of cyber threat and incident data with information technology and operational technology service providers. This proposed amendment aims to enable more thorough information sharing, which will strengthen cybersecurity defenses. FAR case 2021-019, the second regulation, aims to harmonize cybersecurity contractual requirements for unclassified federal information systems throughout federal agencies. This effort aims to guarantee consistency in cybersecurity policies and the protection of unclassified government information systems by instituting uniform rules. These proposed regulations are a reflection of ongoing efforts within the federal government to tighten cybersecurity policy and regulation, emphasizing the significance of coordinated actions to successfully handle cyber threats.
https://www.ipohub.org/article/cybersecurity-laws-regulations John Baadsgaard Since cyberattacks are a common concern and data breaches can have serious implications, it is imperative that organizations of all sizes and sectors ensure cybersecurity and privacy compliance. Due to their low resources, small businesses are especially susceptible to these kinds of dangers, which makes them desirable targets for hackers looking for simple access. These companies run a great deal of danger without strong cybersecurity safeguards in place, including the possibility of suffering monetary losses and reputational damage. Strict privacy laws also need significant investments in cybersecurity to safeguard private data from illegal access, particularly in sectors like healthcare. Moreover, there is mounting pressure on publicly traded corporations to improve their cybersecurity measures in order to thwart security breaches that can enable illicit activities like insider trading and protect market stability and shareholder interests.
